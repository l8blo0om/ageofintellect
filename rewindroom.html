The Rewind Room

Production manual and production tracker for the AI-assisted video pipeline. Everything Moisés delivers gets logged here — labeled by shot, not by content, so nothing gets lost in translation.
01 — Naming Convention

Label the shot. Not what's in it.

Every deliverable gets an ID in the form Section.Shot — nothing else. The section is the story beat; the shot is its position inside that beat.

Don't"Final 10 seconds — arena wide shot, crowd roar, slow push in on scoreboard"
Do3.15

The description lives in the notes field, not the filename or the ID. If you need to know what's in the shot, look it up — the ID's only job is to tell you where it goes.

Rule of thumb: if the label answers "what does this look like," it's wrong. It should only answer "where does this go."
02 — Handoff Protocol

IKEA, not improvisation.

Raw generations come in unordered and unlabeled. The job of production is to take whatever was generated, pop it into the numbered slot it belongs in, and hand back an ordered set — not to rename, re-describe, or reinterpret it.

  1. Every asset gets a shot ID and a version tag on delivery: 3.15 v1, 3.15 v2.
  2. Multiple takes of the same shot stay grouped under the same ID, differentiated only by version.
  3. No editorializing in the label. "This is the one where she's angry" is a note, not a name.

When labels are inconsistent, review becomes guesswork. When they're consistent, review is just: does 3.15 look right — yes or no.

03 — Version Control & Keepers

Consistency beats quantity.

Because prompts vary slightly across generations, wardrobe, framing, and continuity will drift between versions of the same shot. The keeper is the version that matches its neighbors — not necessarily the "best" single image in isolation.

Example from production: three takes shared the same maroon blouse and consistent framing — those became the keepers. Everything that broke continuity got tossed, even if individually well-generated.

Toss aggressively. A shot that's technically good but breaks continuity with the shots around it costs more than it saves.

04 — Workflow & Throughput

Design your workflow, not just your prompts.

AI render/export time is not your time. Whenever a model is generating, upscaling, or exporting, keep the rest of the pipeline moving instead of waiting on one screen.

  • Desktop browser — deep research, long-form writing, document assembly.
  • Desktop app — production chat, prompt refinement, reference management.
  • Phone — voice capture, quick notes, reviewing renders on the go.
  • Generators — rendering, upscaling, exporting run in the background while the above continues.

Match the tool to the task, not the task to whatever's already open. Switching because a process is rendering and another task is ready is orchestration; switching every thirty seconds because of a notification is just distraction. Keep the difference in mind.

05 — Definition of Done

Status pipeline, in order.

  • Not Started — on the shot list, nothing generated yet.
  • In Progress — actively being generated or assembled.
  • Delivered — Moisés has handed it off for review.
  • Needs Revision — reviewed, continuity or quality issue flagged, sent back with notes.
  • Approved — locked. Goes into the final cut.

Nothing skips a stage. "Delivered" is not "Approved" — a shot only leaves the pipeline once it's explicitly marked approved in the tracker.

06 — Work Hacks

Session hygiene keeps the pipeline sane.

  • Rename sessions and chats. The moment a session's purpose is clear, rename it — "Miracle on Ice — Shot 3.15 revisions" beats "New chat" every time. You and Moisés both need to find the right thread in a list of fifty later.
  • Pin what's active. Pin any session you'll return to today or this week. Unpin it once that thread is closed out — pins are for live work, not an archive.
  • If a session is working poorly, don't fight it. Select all, copy the whole project/context, and start a new session with it — or take that same copied context to a different AI entirely. A session that's lost the thread rarely recovers; restarting clean is faster than coaxing it back.
  • Always version as v1, v2, v3. Every file, every render, every export gets a version number — never overwrite, never reuse one. This is the same rule as Section 03, restated here because it's a habit, not just a filing rule.
  • Copy and paste across Apple devices. With Handoff on (same Apple ID, Wi-Fi + Bluetooth on, on all devices), copy on one device and paste on another — copy a prompt on iPhone, paste it into the desktop app; copy a link on Mac, paste it into Notes on iPad. No emailing or messaging yourself text just to move it one device over.
  • Suffix the session name with what's blocking it. Beyond a descriptive name, tack on the stopping point — Shot 3.15 — needs vid URLs. The second you reopen the list you know exactly where you paused and what the next action is, without rereading the whole thread.
When output degrades, the fix usually isn't a better prompt — it's a fresh session with the same context carried over by hand.
07 — Caption Style Lock

One caption style. Every video.

Captions run in ALL CAPS, in a yellow retro caption treatment — the same look on every video in the series, no exceptions.

State the caption style explicitly at the top of every new session or handoff, even though it feels repetitive. Style drift between separately-generated shots is the easiest continuity break to introduce, and the hardest to notice until the videos sit side by side.
